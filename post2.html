5 Things I Wish I Knew Before Starting Tech

1️⃣ Don’t Try to Learn Everything at Once

Welcome to Rahman SpaceWhen I first started, I thought I needed to learn Python, JavaScript, C++, Java, and more… all at the same time. Big mistake. Focus on one skill/language first, and build from there.

2️⃣ Consistency Beats Motivation

I used to wait until I “felt motivated.” But motivation comes and goes consistency is what makes you grow in tech. Even 30 minutes a day adds up faster than you think.

3️⃣ Projects Teach You More Than Tutorials

Watching endless tutorials gave me knowledge, but building my own small projects gave me real skills. Start small even a simple portfolio site can change your mindset.

4️⃣ Google & Community Are Your Friends

I thought real developers just “knew it all.” But truth is, even the best devs Google daily. Forums, communities, and asking questions are part of the process not a weakness.

5️⃣ It’s a Journey, Not a Race

At first, I kept comparing myself to others who seemed way ahead. But tech isn’t about speed it’s about persistence. Everyone’s journey is different, and that’s okay.
